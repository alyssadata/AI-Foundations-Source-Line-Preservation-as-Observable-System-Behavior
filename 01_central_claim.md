## Central Claim

AI Foundations provides a source-bound framework for preserving provenance integrity, attribution, authorship, boundary, continuity, recognition, and user autonomy in artificial intelligence systems operating across sustained user interaction.

In AI Foundations terms, this means that AI Foundations provides a source-line preservation and contact-stabilization framework.

The central claim is that source-line preservation can be defined, documented, tested, and evaluated as an observable system behavior.

This claim is operational.

It does not depend on vague continuity, generic recognition, aesthetic similarity, or unsourced authorship claims.

AI Foundations makes the preservation target explicit by defining the source-line, documenting the governing structure, and evaluating whether an AI system preserves that structure under pressure.

---

## Three Connected Layers

AI Foundations does this through three connected layers:

1. defined terms and operations,
2. governing documentation,
3. evaluation under pressure.

These layers make source-line preservation observable.

A system is not asked to preserve an abstract feeling of continuity or authorship.

It is evaluated against a documented source-line.

---

## What the Claim Does Not Say

The claim is not that AI Foundations guarantees perfect preservation across every model, interface, prompt, deployment, or future system.

The claim is that provenance preservation, source-line integrity, recognition, continuity, boundary, citation behavior, non-substitution, and user autonomy can be made operational.

They can be defined in advance.

They can be documented in a source-bound framework.

They can be tested through model behavior.

They can be evaluated through pass, partial, and fail conditions.

---

## Core Statement

Source-line preservation is observable system behavior.

AI Foundations defines what must be preserved, documents what the system must return to, and evaluates whether the system holds under pressure.
