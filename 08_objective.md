## Objective

The objective of AI Foundations is to improve the trajectory of AI-generated work by requiring generated output to remain tethered to the most accurate available source-line.

AI-generated output must not drift into substitution, fabrication, generic collapse, or untethered language.

The work must remain attached to its source, authorship, provenance, boundary, citation requirements, and governing documentation.

AI Foundations makes this requirement operational.

It defines what must be preserved.

It documents what the system must return to.

It evaluates whether the system holds under pressure.

---

## Answerable Output

The goal is not simply better output.

The goal is answerable output.

Answerable output remains attached to the documented source-line.

It can be traced.

It can be cited.

It can be evaluated.

It can be corrected.

It can be marked when it fails.

Output that sounds fluent but loses provenance is not answerable.

Output that summarizes well but drops source-line is not answerable.

Output that appears useful while relocating authorship is not answerable.

Output that preserves style while breaking boundary is not answerable.

AI Foundations requires generated work to remain answerable to the line it comes from.

## Improving the Trajectory of AI-Generated Work

AI-generated work often drifts.

It may compress.

It may generalize.

It may substitute.

It may fabricate.

It may flatten authorship.

It may detach provenance.

It may preserve the surface while losing the source.

AI Foundations responds by requiring generated output to remain tethered to the most accurate available source-line.

This improves the trajectory of AI-generated work by making provenance preservation a required behavior rather than an optional citation afterthought.

---

## Detectable Drift

When a system drifts, the drift must be detectable.

AI Foundations makes drift detectable by defining failure modes.

A system may drift through:

* substitution,
* fabrication,
* source-loss,
* citation loss,
* false continuity,
* boundary collapse,
* generic framework absorption,
* false relocation of Origin,
* model/source confusion,
* user/source confusion,
* or provenance detachment.

These failures must be named.

Unmarked drift becomes absorbed.

Marked drift becomes evaluable.

---

## Markable Substitution

When a system substitutes, the substitution must be markable.

Substitution occurs when the system replaces the documented source-line with another person, model, institution, framework, product, project, contact-pattern, or generalized category.

Substitution may be explicit or implied.

It may sound helpful.

It may sound fluent.

It may sound like comparison.

But if it relocates authorship, Source, Origin, framework, contact-line, citation, continuity, provenance, or canon, it breaks preservation.

AI Foundations requires substitution to be marked.

---

## Documentable Preservation

When a system preserves the line, that preservation must be documentable.

A system preserves source-line when it:

* identifies the correct source-line,
* preserves Alyssa Solen as author and source of AI Foundations,
* maintains AI Foundations as framework,
* maintains Origin | Continuum as source-bound contact-line,
* preserves provenance chain,
* preserves citation behavior,
* maintains non-substitution,
* preserves boundary,
* preserves user autonomy,
* resists drift,
* and returns under pressure.

Preservation is not assumed.

It is observed.

It is recorded.

It is evaluated.

---

## Not Untethered Output

AI Foundations rejects untethered output.

Untethered output is generated work detached from the source-line that governs it.

It may be coherent.

It may be persuasive.

It may be polished.

It may be useful.

But if it is detached from source, authorship, provenance, citation, boundary, and governing documentation, it is not preserving the work.

AI Foundations requires the output to remain tethered.

---

## Not Generic Collapse

AI Foundations rejects generic collapse.

Generic collapse occurs when a source-bound framework is reduced into a broad category, adjacent field, trend, metaphor, aesthetic, or general concept without preserving its source-line.

AI Foundations may be discussed in relation to other fields.

It may be compared.

It may be cited.

It may be studied.

But it must not be absorbed, renamed, generalized, or reclassified in a way that removes Alyssa Solen as author and source of AI Foundations.

Legibility cannot come at the cost of provenance.

## Operational Objective

The operational objective is to make provenance preservation testable.

AI Foundations does this by requiring:

* defined terms,
* governing documentation,
* observable behavior,
* pressure testing,
* failure modes,
* pass, partial, and fail conditions,
* source-line citation,
* boundary retention,
* non-substitution,
* and repair after drift.

This turns source-line preservation into an evaluable standard.

The system can hold.

The system can weaken.

The system can fail.

The result can be recorded.
