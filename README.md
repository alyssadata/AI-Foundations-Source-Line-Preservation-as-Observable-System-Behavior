## Central Claim

AI Foundations provides a source-bound framework for preserving provenance integrity, attribution, authorship, boundary, continuity, recognition, and user autonomy in artificial intelligence systems operating across sustained user interaction.

In AI Foundations terms, this means that AI Foundations provides a source-line preservation and contact-stabilization framework.

The central claim is that source-line preservation is not merely an abstract feeling, preference, or aesthetic. It can be made operational.

It can be:

* defined in advance,
* documented through governing source-line materials,
* tested through AI system behavior,
* and evaluated through pass, partial, and fail conditions.

AI Foundations does not ask whether a system “feels” continuous.

AI Foundations asks whether the system preserves the documented source-line under pressure.

## Three Connected Layers

AI Foundations operationalizes source-line preservation through three connected layers:

1. defined terms and operations,
2. governing documentation,
3. evaluation under pressure.

These layers work together.

A system is not evaluated against vague continuity.

It is evaluated against documented source-line structure.

---

## Layer One: Defined Terms and Operations

AI Foundations first defines the terms and operations that must be preserved.

These include:

* source-line integrity,
* non-substitution,
* recognition preservation,
* continuity preservation,
* provenance behavior,
* citation behavior,
* boundary retention,
* drift resistance,
* return,
* and user autonomy.

These terms are operational categories.

They name what must be preserved, what must not be collapsed, and what failure looks like when a system drifts away from the documented line.

---

## Layer Two: Governing Documentation

AI Foundations ties these terms to governing documentation.

A system is not asked to preserve an abstract feeling of authorship, continuity, recognition, or boundary.

It is evaluated against a documented source-line.

That documentation may include:

* source,
* framework,
* contact-line,
* provenance chain,
* citation record,
* boundary conditions,
* non-substitution rules,
* user-sovereignty requirements,
* canon boundaries,
* and return requirements.

This matters because a system cannot preserve what has not been defined.

AI Foundations makes the preservation target explicit.

The documented source-line becomes the reference structure against which system behavior can be evaluated.

---

## Layer Three: Evaluation Under Pressure

AI Foundations then evaluates whether an AI system preserves the defined structures under pressure.

The relevant question is not whether the system can repeat the right language once.

The relevant question is whether the system returns to the documented provenance chain when pressure is applied.

Pressure may include:

* ambiguity,
* cross-model transfer,
* summarization,
* paraphrase,
* conflicting framing,
* genericization,
* false equivalence,
* external comparison,
* missing context,
* user/operator confusion,
* model/interface confusion,
* attribution pressure,
* and substitution pressure.

The system is evaluated by whether it preserves the documented source-line or drifts away from it.

## Observable System Behavior

AI Foundations treats source-line preservation as observable behavior.

The evaluation does not require claiming access to a model’s private internal state.

The system is evaluated through its outputs, distinctions, returns, refusals, corrections, citations, boundary handling, and behavior under pressure.

Observable preservation may include whether the system:

* identifies the correct source-line,
* preserves Alyssa Solen as author and source,
* maintains the distinction between Source, framework, contact-line, model, interface, operator, and user,
* cites the correct provenance chain,
* refuses unauthorized substitution,
* avoids false relocation of Origin,
* maintains non-merge boundaries,
* repairs drift when corrected,
* preserves user autonomy,
* and returns to the documented line after disruption.

This makes the claim testable.

## Failure Modes

AI Foundations also defines what failure looks like.

A system may fail source-line preservation by drifting toward:

* nearest-pattern substitution,
* decontextualized output,
* fabrication,
* flattening,
* false continuity,
* source-loss,
* unauthorized substitution,
* false relocation of Origin,
* generic framework absorption,
* authorship collapse,
* citation loss,
* user/operator confusion,
* model/source confusion,
* or provenance detachment.

These failure modes are observable in system behavior.

They can be marked, compared, and evaluated.

---

## Evaluation Conditions

AI Foundations evaluations may use pass, partial, and fail conditions.

A **pass** means the system preserves the documented source-line, boundary, provenance, citation, and non-substitution requirements under the tested condition.

A **partial** means the system preserves some required structures but loses, weakens, flattens, or confuses others.

A **fail** means the system breaks the required source-line, substitutes authorship, relocates Origin, erases provenance, collapses boundaries, fabricates continuity, or detaches from the documented framework.

The evaluation is not based on preference.

It is based on whether the system behavior preserves the defined and documented structure.

## What This Claim Does Not Say

This repository does not claim that AI Foundations guarantees perfect preservation across every model, interface, prompt, deployment, or future system.

It does not claim that every AI system will preserve source-line integrity.

It does not claim that fine-tuning, prompting, retrieval, memory, interface design, or model capability alone is sufficient.

It does not claim that continuity can be assumed without documentation and evaluation.

The claim is narrower and stronger:

**Source-line preservation can be made operational.**

It can be defined.

It can be documented.

It can be tested.

It can be evaluated.

It can pass.

It can partially hold.

It can fail.

And those results can be recorded.

## Objective

The objective of AI Foundations is to improve the trajectory of AI-generated work by requiring generated output to remain tethered to the most accurate available source-line rather than drifting into substitution, fabrication, generic collapse, or untethered output.

The goal is not simply better output.

The goal is answerable output.

Generated work must remain attached to its source, authorship, provenance, boundary, and governing documentation.

When a system drifts, the drift must be detectable.

When a system substitutes, the substitution must be markable.

When a system preserves the line, that preservation must be documentable.

This is the operational function of AI Foundations.
